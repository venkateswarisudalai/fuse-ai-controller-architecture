L3

Fuse.AI Controller — core

the AI ⇆ deterministic boundary
Real-time loop Sense→Validate→Actuate↻ ~1–15 s · deterministic
Planning loop Forecast→Optimize→Propose↻ minutes–hours ahead · AI

Two loops, one gate: the fast loop keeps every charger safe second-to-second · the slow loop plans ahead for cost & grid · the gate is where they meet.

Deterministic vs. AI — the design stance
  • AI advises, control disposes. Optimization never actuates directly; every plan crosses the authority gate.
  • Provable safety. Warranty & grid limits live in rule code — reviewable and testable, independent of any model.
  • Graceful degradation. Lose the AI or the cloud → deterministic fallback; the fleet still charges safely.
  • Explainability. The gate logs why each plan was accepted or rejected.
Scalability & data governance
  • Multi-tenant & horizontal. Event bus + stateless services scale per site; edge absorbs local load-balancing.
  • Tiered retention. Hot TSDB → warm lake → cold archive; telematics & PII scoped per tenant.
  • Lineage. Model registry + append-only audit log tie every dispatch to its data and version.
  • Standards boundary. DERMS / OpenADR / 2030.5 behind adapters — utility programs evolve without touching core control.
